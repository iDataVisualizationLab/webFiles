Classifying Tree Layouts

  1. Hierarchical relationship: Node-link vs. containment vs. stacking
    • Node-link (drawing a link)
    • Containment (nesting children within the parent)
    • Stacking (having a spatial area of the child touching its parent)

  2. Shape preservation: Stable vs. malleable structure
    How a subtree appears is affected by the structure of the tree and where it locates in the tree

  3. Space-centric filling: Root-centric vs. parent-centric
    For example, Radial Layout is root-centric since the distribution of leaf nodes are computed with respect to the root node

  4. Bundling angularity: Wide turns vs. sharp turns.

User Study



Visualization Tasks

  • Through discussions with systems biologists, taxonomists, and ontology researchers, we identified two primary tasks important for visual exploration of hierarchical datasets:

    • T1: Effectively characterize hierarchical structure
      In taxonomic and ontological alignment domains, hierarchies can get extremely complicated. More importantly, these classifications can be contentious, and they may change from year to year as new discoveries or interpretations are made [13]

    • T2: Minimize ambiguity introduced by edge bundlingWhen HEB is applied, tracing a bundled link can lead to the perception of incorrect connectivity.
      For some tree layouts the loss in detail can be amplified depending on various factors, such as the chosen visual encodings and the overall structure of the tree.

Our controlled experiments

  • 14 subjects (11 males and 3 females), aged between 22 and 38

  • The total length of the study (including two experiments) ran from between 30 minutes to one hour depending on the speed of the participant

  • We collected accuracy and completion time for each quantitative task; user preference data were also collected at the end
